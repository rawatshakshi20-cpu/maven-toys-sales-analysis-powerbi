# maven-toys-sales-analysis-powerbi

📊 Maven Toys Sales Analysis – Power BI Dashboard

📌 Project Overview

This project presents an interactive Power BI dashboard analyzing sales performance for Maven Toys, a retail toy store chain.

The objective is to monitor revenue, profitability, product performance, and store-level trends to support strategic business decisions.




Business Questions:




How is the overall business performance?

Which cities generate the highest sales revenue?

How does store location impact sales performance?

What is the revenue composition by product category and which categories are the highest volume drivers?

Which product category generates the highest profit and is it consistent across store locations?

What are the monthly sales trends and seasonal patterns?

Which stores operates effficiently?

Which products contribute the most to overall profitability?



🎨 Dashboard Design & Visualization Strategy
Page 1: Executive Summary (High-Level Overview)

KPI Cards: Instant tracking of Total Sales, Profit, and Margin %.

Line chart (YoY Comparison): Implemented a Line Chart to track monthly sales trends across 2022 and 2023, identifying seasonal peaks and growth trajectory.

Map Visual: A Bubble Map to provide a geographic view of revenue distribution across Mexico.

Donut Chart: I used a Donut Chart to show the proportion of sales across different store types (Downtown, Airport, etc.), making it easy to see which location archetype dominates the market.

Page 2: Store & Product Performance (Granular Analysis)

Bar Chart (Units Sold): A Bar Chart for Units Sold by Product Category to identify which categories drive the highest volume and foot traffic

Combo Chart (Profit & Profit Margin): A Column Chart is used to compare Profit and Profit Margin across product categories. This is crucial for seeing if high-profit categories  are also the most efficient ones.

Performance Matrices: Detailed tables for Stores and Products using Conditional Formatting (Icons) to flag growth outliers and high-margin items.



Key Insights

1. The business generated $14M in revenue and $4.01M in profit, resulting in a 27.79% profit margin with 1M units sold.
However, sales grew 30.86% while profit grew only 16.04%, indicating rising costs or increased sales from lower-margin products, higher operational costs, or promotional pricing strategies.

2. Most revenue is generated in large cities such as Mexico City and Guadalajara, highlighting the importance of dense urban markets for toy demand.

3. Downtown locations contribute ~57% of total revenue (~$8M), significantly outperforming Commercial, Residential, and Airport stores due to higher foot traffic.

4.Toys generate the highest revenue (~$5M), making them the core category driving overall sales followed by Arts and Crafts. However, Art & Crafts leads in total units sold, indicating a high-volume but lower-price product segment. While Toys drive revenue, Electronics show the highest profit margin (~45%), making them the most profitable category per sale.

6.Toys generate the highest total profit among all product categories, contributing approximately $1M in profit overall. Profitability differs by store location. While Toys generate the highest profit in Downtown and Residential stores, Electronics lead profitability in Commercial and Airport locations indicating location-specific purchasing behavior.

7. The top products by profit include Colorbuds, Action Figure, Deck of Cards, Lego Bricks, and Magic Sand, indicating that a small number of products contribute significantly to overall profitability.
Lego Bricks generate the highest revenue (~$2.38M) but have a low profit margin (~12.5%), meaning they drive volume but not profitability.
Colorbuds combine high revenue (~$2.19M) with a very high margin (~53%), making them one of the most valuable products for the business.

8. A small number of stores contribute a large share of revenue, with top stores include locations such as Ciudad de Mexico and Guadalajara, with Ciudad de Mexico 2 generating the highest revenue (~$554K).
Most of the top-performing stores also maintain profit margins between ~25%–30%, indicating healthy profitability alongside strong sales.
while most stores show positive revenue growth, Guadalajara – Store 4 shows negative growth (-4.24%) indicating potential local demand or operational challenges that may require further investigation.

9. Sales peak around March and December, while demand dips during late summer months, likely driven by holiday shopping and gift purchases  indicating clear seasonality in toy purchases. Comparing years, 2023 consistently outperforms 2022 across most months, showing stronger sales momentum and overall business growth over time.
